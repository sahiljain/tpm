Checklist

This checklist is provided to make sure you haven't missed any important details. For further clarification, see the individual pages.

General

  • [  ] I have read the http://www.ece.uwaterloo.ca/~tppe000/Requirements/
  • [  ] I have watched the example talks
  • [  ] I attended the presentation On Technical Presentations and read through the Guidelines
  • [  ] I have downloaded the presentation template and at least considered using it
  • [  ] I have printed out two copies of the TPPE Evaluation Form, read it, filled in the relevant information at the top, and will bring them to the session

Organization

  • [  ] I have prepared my presentation and saved it on a functioning USB flash drive
  • [  ] If my presentation is not in .ppt, .pptx, or .pdf format, I will be bringing my own computer which has a VGA analogue display connector (required)
  • [  ] My talk is between 12 and 15 minutes (required)
  • [  ] I have given sufficient background to familiarize the audience with the subject
  • [  ] I have explained all non-obvious abbreviations and acronyms
  • [  ] My talk is not a high-level overview of some technology

Slides

  • [  ] The slides are in either .ppt, .pptx or .pdf format (recommended)
  • [  ] I have a title slide (required)
  • [  ] I have an outline slide (required)
  • [  ] I have a summary and conclusions slide (required)
  • [  ] I have approximately one slide per minute
  • [  ] The slides are numbered
  • [  ] I have no slides with more than 60 words on it
  • [  ] I do not have a slide which covers two or more different topics
  • [  ] I have at least one table (recommended)
  • [  ] I have at least one graphic (recommended), especially where it is useful
  • [  ] My font size is reasonable (24-32 pt for main text)
  • [  ] The text on my slides is a brief summary of what I am saying (and does not contain full sentences)

Presentation

  • [  ] I am not talking in a monotone voice
  • [  ] I am not spending a significant amount of time reading either the slides on the computer or my notes
  • [  ] I am looking the audience members in the eye
  • [  ] I am not saying um or ah (or some other mumbling noise which could be substituted by silence)
  • [  ] I do not put my hands into my pockets
  • [  ] I am not standing like a cardboard cut-out
  • [  ] I am not playing with my watch, my zipper, etc.

Questions

  • [  ] I have practiced saying "I do not know." and will use it appropriately
  • [  ] I will not make up answers
